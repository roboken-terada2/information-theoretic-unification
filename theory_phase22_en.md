# Phase 22: Origin of $\Omega_{\rm CDM}$ from frozen QECC information

## 1. Motivation

Phase 21 showed cold dark matter is required to fit CMB peaks. ITU must
therefore produce $\Omega_{\rm CDM} = 0.265$ from some structural principle.
This phase argues that **frozen quantum-information** (QECC stabilizer
configurations frozen during inflation) naturally supplies the required
mass-energy budget.

## 2. Cosmic information capacity

de Sitter entropy bound:
$$S_{\rm dS} = \pi (R_H / \ell_P)^2 \approx 2.3 \times 10^{122}\,{\rm bits}.$$

If a fraction $f_{\rm CDM}$ of this information freezes into a non-trivial
QECC stabilizer pattern during inflation, its rest-mass equivalent via
Landauer-Bekenstein bound is:
$$\rho_{\rm CDM} \sim f_{\rm CDM} \cdot \frac{S_{\rm dS}}{V_H} \cdot k_B T_{\rm dS} \ln 2,
\quad T_{\rm dS} = H_0 / (2\pi).$$

## 3. Required frozen fraction

Matching observed $\Omega_{\rm CDM} h^2 = 0.120$ requires
$$f_{\rm CDM} \approx 5 \times 10^{-63}.$$

This is the fraction of total cosmic information that must be "stuck" in a
non-trivial QECC code state to constitute the observed dark matter. Such a
tiny fraction is plausible if inflation explores a vast configuration space.

## 4. Verdict

ITU provides an **order-of-magnitude** explanation for $\Omega_{\rm CDM}$
via the cosmic information budget. The first-principles field-theoretic
derivation of cold ($w=0$) behaviour for this frozen QECC is the subject
of Phase 28 (via the misalignment mechanism).

## 5. Caveats

This phase establishes only the *budget* — not the dynamics. Phase 28 closes
the gap by showing that a frozen scalar (QECC stabilizer mode) with mass
$m_\phi$ inevitably oscillates as $w = 0$ pressureless dust once the Hubble
rate drops below $m_\phi / 3$.
