## Abstract

This is the fifth Pass-1.5 paper (Tier 1+ #5) — a 16-phase operator-algebraic deep dive into cancer biology. We propose **K_cancer = -log ρ_tumor** as the modular Hamiltonian of tumor cell heterogeneity, providing a quantitative ITU-derived metric that unifies clonal evolution, immune evasion, CAR-T resistance, CRISPR therapy, metastasis, single-cell RNA-seq atlases, mRNA cancer vaccines, and liquid biopsy under a single operator-algebraic framework.

Four core hypotheses (H_O1-H_O4):

- **H_O1**: Tumor is operator-algebraic state — ρ_tumor over cell mutation/expression profiles.
- **H_O2**: ⟨K_cancer⟩ quantifies malignancy — benign tumors have low entropy (1 dominant clone), malignant tumors have high entropy (multiple subclones).
- **H_O3**: Treatment response ⇔ K_cancer modular flow — resistance emergence = K_cancer increase; complete remission = K_cancer → 0.
- **H_O4**: Pan-Cancer Atlas reveals universal K_cancer signatures across 33+ cancer types.
