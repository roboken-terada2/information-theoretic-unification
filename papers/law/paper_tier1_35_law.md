## Abstract

We apply the Information-Theoretic Unification (ITU) framework to **law and jurisprudence**, the third paper of Block C (social/humanities/arts), reaching the half-completion mark of Block C. We introduce **K_law** across **8 sub-states** — constitutional, criminal, civil, international, human rights, AI ethics, IP, procedural — and verify the ITU axiom δS = δ⟨K⟩ to machine precision (rel_err = 0) in **11 contexts**.

**Theoretical contribution**: Law operates as a K-state normalization mechanism. Constitutional law forms the top prior; criminal/civil law regulate norm-violation and private agreements; procedural law sets game rules; international law coordinates national K-states; AI ethics builds new K-state attractors for the digital age.
